Hosting & Emceeing

I'm the person you hand the mic to when you want the room to feel alive — and still run on time.

There's a specific kind of magic when a room is hosted well. People relax. The awkward gaps disappear. Strangers start leaning in instead of checking their phones. That's the job I love — being the warm, slightly cheeky voice that holds a room of 80 together and makes a programme feel like an experience, not an agenda.

I've hosted company events, emceed NUS N-House Demo Day in front of judges and founders, and moderated founder sharing sessions built to pull real, useful conversation out of busy people. Whether it's keeping the energy high or asking the one question that makes a panel honest, I keep things human.

Every room I host is really just me creating connection — in real time, out loud.
