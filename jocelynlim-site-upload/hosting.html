Hosting & Emceeing

I'm the person you hand the mic to when you want a room to feel alive — and still run on time.

There's a specific kind of magic when a room is hosted well. People relax. The awkward gaps disappear. Strangers stop checking their phones and start leaning in. That's the part I love — being the warm, sometimes slightly cheeky voice that holds a room together and makes a programme feel like an experience, not an agenda.

I've hosted an 80-person company event, emceed NUS N-House Demo Day in front of judges and founders, and moderated founder sharing sessions built to pull real conversation out of busy people. I was also awarded Best Speaker across my entire business cohort at Ngee Ann Polytechnic, and earned an A in both my Public Speaking and Business Communication for Leaders modules at NUS. I'm early in this — but give me a run sheet and a room, and I'll make sure people actually remember being there.

The rooms I love to hold: demo days, panels, corporate town-halls, conferences, product launches, and founder Q&As — anything where the energy and the timing both matter.
