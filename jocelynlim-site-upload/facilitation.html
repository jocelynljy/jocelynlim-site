Facilitation

Guiding people — and the young leaders among them — to the moment it finally clicks.

Hosting is holding a room. Facilitation, for me, is something quieter: guiding. I love the slow work of walking alongside someone until they reach their own aha — the moment a goal gets clear, or a shy kid realises they can lead.

Right now I'm part of a project working with children to build a community space — and my favourite part is mentoring the young leaders who'll guide the rest of the group to build it themselves. In another, I worked with youths on their next steps in life: goal-setting, and the bigger question of what they actually want.

From children to youths, the job is the same — create a space safe enough for honesty, then guide people gently toward what they're capable of. Safe, playful, intentional. That's the whole method.

🔒
Because most of this work is with schools and young people, I keep real session photos private under NDA. The images on this page are AI-generated illustrations of the kind of space I try to create — not actual participants.
Facilitation is me guiding people — to each other, and to what they're capable of.
