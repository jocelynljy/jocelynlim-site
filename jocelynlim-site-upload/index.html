the why

Lots of side quests. One reason.

If you scrolled through my week, it honestly wouldn't make much sense. One day I'm holding a mic in front of a room of founders. The next I'm cutting pipe cleaners into petals at my kitchen table, or sketching out an app to help people remember the friends they keep meaning to text.

Here's the thread I didn't notice for years: I'm not chasing job titles, I'm chasing the same feeling. That moment a stranger turns into a person. The room that goes quiet because someone finally said the real thing.

We're all a little more online — and a little more alone — than we used to be. So everything I make is a small rebellion against that: a way to keep the human parts alive. Meet people. Go deeper. Stay connected.

the human behind it

Hi, I'm Jocelyn.

I studied Marketing at NUS Business School, but the real education happened between countries — growing up across Singapore, Indonesia and Canada, doing homestays and farm stays through ASEAN youth exchanges, and living alone in Toronto for a startup stint that taught me more than any classroom could.

I don't travel to collect stamps. I travel because I'm endlessly curious about how people live, connect and make meaning — and that same curiosity is the muscle behind everything on this page.
