# Make Them a Character — Prompt Guide

Turn anyone (a friend, your partner, your whole group, even yourself) into a cute hand-drawn marker chibi character. Copy the style base, fill in the template with their real-life vibe, and paste it into an AI image tool.

---

## 1. The style base (the secret sauce)

Every prompt should start with this. It locks in the "marker on cream paper" look so all your characters match:

> A cute hand-drawn cartoon chibi character illustration. Use clean, confident black marker linework on an off-white cream paper background with a subtle paper texture. Use simple flat colour fills (no gradients) for hair and specific props only. Add a soft light-pink blush on the cheeks. The drawing should feel like a professional artist's quick marker doodle: charming, slightly messy but intentional, and full of personality.

---

## 2. The character template

Copy this, then fill in the `[ ]` bits with the person's details:

> [paste the style base above first]
>
> Character Name: [name]
>
> Anatomy & Pose: A solo full-body chibi character with a large round head (about half the total height) and a small expressive body. Standing in a [pose, e.g. twerking, mid-spin, shielding their head, bouncing] pose that captures their energy.
>
> Face & Expression: [expression, e.g. cheeky wink with tongue out, nonchalant 'heh' smirk, heart-eyes with a little drool]. Include details like [glasses, big teeth, dimples].
>
> Hair: [colour] hair in a [style, e.g. voluminous butterfly cut, low ponytail, half-up bun]. [detail, e.g. dark roots with lighter tips, side parting].
>
> Outfit: [clothing, e.g. baggy oversized tee and shorts, a classical dance outfit, a ballet tutu]. [accessories, e.g. a party hat, shutter shades, a hair clip].
>
> Props & Vibe: Surround the character with [3 to 4 objects, e.g. a DJ console, a giant hammer, musical notes, a floating bottle, hearts, sparkles].
>
> Text Elements: Include a small handwritten name label '[name]' and [number] speech bubbles with their catchphrases: "[phrase 1]" and "[phrase 2]".

---

## 3. How to turn a real person into a drawing

| If they are... | Draw it as... |
| :--- | :--- |
| A bit of a menace / chaotic | A giant weapon, or a split "angel + devil" face |
| Always dancing / high energy | A dynamic pose (twerk, spin, bounce) |
| Always laughing | Eye-smiles (^^) and hands squishing their cheeks |
| Obsessed with someone or something | Heart-eyes, or a tiny bit of drool |
| Effortlessly chill | Half-lidded eyes and a "heh" smirk |
| Known for one signature habit | 1 to 2 floating "totem" objects that represent it |

---

## 4. Pro-tips

- **Long catchphrase?** Split it across two speech bubbles so it stays clean and doodle-like.
- **Colour sparingly.** Only colour the hair and 1 to 2 key props. Leave the clothes white so the character pops.
- **Always say "cream paper background"** so it doesn't come out on a boring clinical white.
